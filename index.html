About Me

Hi, I’m Mihir! I’m a mathematics and computer science student passionate about algorithms, complexity theory, and their applications in research. I’m beginning my MSc in Mathematics and Foundations of Computer Science at the University of Oxford in Fall 2025, after completing my BS in Mathematics and Computer Science at Haverford College.

Research Projects

  • Project 1: Balancing node centrality in social networks — proved hardness results and developed approximation algorithms. Culminated in a preprint with collaborators at the University of Utah.
  • Project 2: Investigated parameterized algorithms for the 2-edge-connected spanning subgraph problem with Georgetown University’s Algorithms group.
  • Project 3: Improved selection algorithms for finding the kth order statistic (Quickselect pivoting strategies) under supervision at George Washington University.

Teaching & Leadership

I’ve worked as a Teaching Assistant for multiple CS courses at Haverford and Bryn Mawr Colleges, guiding students through algorithms and data structures. I also served as a board member of HaverCode, organizing events, workshops, and hackathons to support the CS community.
