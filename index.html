Some Projects from My Undergrad

Improving Network Fairness With Edge Addition

Assuming information spreads along the edges of a social network, some nodes naturally have greater access to information simply because of their position in the network, creating inherent advantage gaps. The problem I studied asks whether we can add a limited number of edges to reduce these gaps (in particular, to equalize the closeness centralities of two chosen nodes). This turns out to be NP-hard, so my work focused on designing approximation algorithms and proving inapproximability results.

This research formed my undergraduate thesis at Haverford, where I was fortunate to be advised by Dr. Sorelle Friedler (CS) and Dr. Josh Sabloff (Math), and to collaborate with Dr. Blair Sullivan and the Theory in Practice group at the University of Utah. My Math thesis submission is accessible to a general math audience, while the CS thesis submission has a more extensive literature review and assumes familiarity with concepts from an undergraduate algorithms course.
